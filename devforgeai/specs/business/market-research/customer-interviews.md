# Customer Interview Questions

## Hypothesis: Entrepreneurs need structured frameworks to validate business ideas before investing resources

1. How do you currently go about validating whether a business idea is worth pursuing?
2. Tell me about the last time you invested significant time or money into a business idea that did not work out.
3. What information would help you feel more confident before committing resources to a new venture?
4. Describe the process you follow when deciding whether to move forward with or abandon an idea.

## Hypothesis: Solo founders lack access to affordable market research tools and methodologies

1. How do you currently research whether there is demand for your product or service?
2. What tools or resources do you use to understand your target market?
3. Walk me through a recent experience where you tried to gather market data for a business decision.
4. Describe the biggest challenge you face when trying to understand your competitive landscape.

## Hypothesis: Customer interview skills are a gap for technical founders transitioning to entrepreneurship

1. Tell me about your experience conducting customer interviews or user research.
2. How do you decide what questions to ask potential customers about your product idea?
3. What makes it difficult for you to get honest, useful feedback from potential customers?

## Hypothesis: AI-assisted business planning tools can reduce the time from idea to validated concept

1. How long does it typically take you to go from a new business idea to a decision about whether to pursue it?
2. Describe the steps you take when creating a business plan or pitch for a new idea.
3. What parts of the business planning process feel most tedious or time-consuming to you?
4. Tell me about any tools or AI assistants you have used to help with business planning or market research.
