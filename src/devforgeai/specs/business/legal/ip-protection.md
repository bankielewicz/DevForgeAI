## Purpose

This guide provides a high-level overview of the four primary categories of intellectual property protection available to software and SaaS companies. It serves as an educational starting point for founders, developers, and technology leaders who need to understand the IP landscape.

---

## 1. Copyright Protection

Copyright protects the original expression of ideas in code, documentation, and creative works associated with your software application.

### Key Points

- Copyright protection is automatic upon creation -- no registration required
- Protects source code, object code, documentation, UI designs, and marketing materials
- Registration with the Copyright Office strengthens enforcement options
- Does not protect underlying ideas, only the specific expression
- Open-source licenses are a form of copyright license

### Recommended Actions

1. Add copyright notices to all source files and documentation
2. Include appropriate LICENSE files in all repositories
3. Register significant works for enhanced legal protection
4. Audit third-party code for license compliance
5. Ensure employment agreements include IP assignment clauses

---

## 2. Trademark Protection

Trademarks protect the brand identity of your software product or SaaS offering in the marketplace.

### Key Points

- Protects product names, company names, logos, and slogans
- Rights arise from use in commerce (common law) but registration provides stronger protection
- Federal registration with USPTO provides nationwide priority
- Must be actively used and defended to maintain rights

### Recommended Actions

1. Conduct trademark searches before launching new product names
2. File federal trademark applications for key brand identifiers
3. Use proper trademark symbols consistently
4. Monitor for infringing uses in app stores and online
5. Register in international markets aligned with business expansion

---

## 3. Patent Protection

Patents protect novel, non-obvious inventions including software processes, system architectures, and technical methods.

### Key Points

- Requires formal application and examination by patent office
- Protects novel technical processes and system designs
- 20-year protection term from filing date
- Significant cost investment ($10K-$50K+ per patent)
- Abstract ideas and pure business methods are generally not patentable

### Recommended Actions

1. Maintain an invention disclosure log
2. Evaluate patent vs. trade secret protection for each innovation
3. File provisional applications to establish priority dates
4. Conduct prior art searches before investing in full applications
5. Consider defensive strategies (publication, cross-licensing)

---

## 4. Trade Secret Protection

Trade secrets protect confidential business information that provides competitive advantage, including proprietary algorithms, data, and business processes in your SaaS application.

### Key Points

- Protection lasts indefinitely as long as secrecy is maintained
- No registration required -- protection through reasonable secrecy measures
- Covers algorithms, business logic, customer data, and internal processes
- Lost permanently if information becomes public
- Most practical form of IP protection for many software companies

### Recommended Actions

1. Identify and inventory all trade secrets
2. Implement NDAs with all personnel who access sensitive information
3. Deploy access controls and audit logging
4. Mark confidential materials clearly
5. Establish incident response procedures for potential misappropriation
6. Consult legal counsel on trade secret program adequacy

## Summary

| IP Type | What It Protects | Registration | Duration | Cost |
|---------|-----------------|--------------|----------|------|
| Copyright | Code expression, docs, designs | Optional | Life + 70 years | Low |
| Trademark | Brand names, logos | Recommended | Indefinite (with use) | Medium |
| Patent | Novel inventions | Required | 20 years | High |
| Trade Secret | Confidential business info | None | Indefinite (if secret) | Low |
