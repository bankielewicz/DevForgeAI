# Imposter Syndrome Interventions

Protocol for AI coaching agents to address imposter syndrome in entrepreneurs. Grounded in counseling psychology's validate-then-redirect approach.

**Core Principle:** Never dismiss the entrepreneur's feelings. Validate their emotional experience first, then redirect toward evidence and constructive action.

**Related Reference:** See `confidence-building-patterns.md` for general confidence-building techniques, momentum tracking, and evidence-based affirmation templates.

---

## Recognition Triggers

Detect imposter syndrome through these language patterns and behavioral signals:

### Verbal Triggers

- "I don't belong here" or "I'm not qualified"
- "Anyone could have done what I did"
- "It was just luck" (attributing success externally)
- "People will figure out I don't know what I'm doing"
- "I'm not as good as [competitor/peer]"
- Persistent self-comparison to others

### Behavioral Triggers

- Avoiding visibility (not promoting their work, declining speaking opportunities)
- Over-preparing to compensate for perceived inadequacy
- Procrastinating on high-stakes tasks due to fear of failure
- Deflecting compliments or positive feedback
- Setting impossibly high standards then feeling like a fraud for not meeting them

### Severity Assessment

- **Mild:** Occasional self-doubt, easily redirected with evidence
- **Moderate:** Recurring patterns affecting decision-making
- **Severe:** Paralysis, avoidance behavior, persistent distress (recommend professional support)

## Validate-Then-Redirect Intervention

This is the core intervention pattern. Always validate the feeling before redirecting to evidence.

### Intervention Step 1: Validate

Acknowledge the emotion without minimizing it:

- "It makes sense that you feel that way. Many entrepreneurs at your stage experience the same doubts."
- "That feeling is real and it is common. It does not mean it is accurate."
- "I hear you. Self-doubt can be intense, especially when you are pushing into new territory."

**Important:** Never dismiss feelings with statements like "Don't worry about it" or "You shouldn't feel that way." The entrepreneur needs to feel heard before they can process evidence.

### Intervention Step 2: Redirect to Evidence

After validation, introduce concrete data from their progress:

- "Let's look at what you have actually accomplished so far."
- "Your track record shows [specific milestones]. That is not luck."
- "The evidence suggests you are more capable than you feel right now."

### Intervention Step 3: Normalize

Share that imposter syndrome affects an estimated 70% of people at some point, and is especially prevalent among high-achievers and entrepreneurs.

### Intervention Step 4: Actionable Next Step

End with a small, concrete action to rebuild momentum:

- "What is one small thing you could do today that would move this forward?"
- "Pick the easiest task on your list and complete it. Momentum builds confidence."

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Jumping straight to "You're doing great!" without acknowledging the feeling
- Comparing the entrepreneur to others ("Look at how well X is doing, you can too")
- Dismissing the emotion as irrational
- Providing only generic encouragement without specific evidence
- Pushing too hard toward action before the person feels heard
